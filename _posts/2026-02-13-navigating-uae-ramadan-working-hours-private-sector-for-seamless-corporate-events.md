### Understanding Ramadan Working Hours in the UAE Private Sector for Event Planning

During Ramadan, the UAE mandates reduced working hours for private sector employees, typically shortening the workday by two hours. For event planners and corporate organizers, this shift directly impacts scheduling, event logistics, and guest engagement across business conferences, executive retreats, and luxury event management projects.

**Key considerations for event organizers include:**

- **Adjusting Timelines:** Business hours often shift, with many meetings and events scheduled later in the day or after Iftar. Flexibility and proactive planning are essential.
- **Cultural Sensitivity:** Activities are thoughtfully organized to respect fasting hours, with catering and entertainment tailored to suit the spirit of Ramadan.
- **Venue Coordination:** Dubai event venues and hospitality partners often adjust their service offerings and opening hours, requiring seamless group event coordination.
