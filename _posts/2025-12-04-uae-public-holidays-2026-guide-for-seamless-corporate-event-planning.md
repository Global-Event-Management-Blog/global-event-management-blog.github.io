## UAE Public Holidays 2026: Key Dates to Know

Aligning your event strategy with the UAE’s national holidays ensures maximum attendance, seamless logistics, and unforgettable guest experiences. The following are projected public holidays for 2026 (subject to official lunar calendar confirmations):

- **New Year’s Day**: Thursday, 1 January  
- **Eid Al Fitr**: Likely Monday, 16 March to Thursday, 19 March  
- **Arafat Day**: Likely Wednesday, 27 May  
- **Eid Al Adha**: Likely Thursday, 28 May to Saturday, 30 May  
- **Islamic New Year**: Likely Tuesday, 18 August  
- **Prophet Muhammad’s Birthday**: Likely Monday, 27 April  
- **Commemoration Day**: Monday, 30 November  
- **UAE National Day**: Tuesday, 2 December and Wednesday, 3 December  

*Note: Islamic holiday dates are subject to moon sighting and may shift.*

## How Public Holidays Shape Corporate Events in Dubai and Beyond

The UAE’s public holidays are not just days off—they are vibrant celebrations of heritage, faith, and unity. For corporate event organizers and global brands, these dates present both opportunities and planning considerations:

- **Venue Availability:** High demand near public holidays means premier Dubai event venues and luxury hospitality partners are booked early. Advance group travel planning is vital.
- **Cultural Experiences:** Incorporating local traditions into your program—such as Iftar gatherings during Ramadan or National Day décor—enhances guest engagement and respect for local culture.
- **Event Logistics:** Adjusting schedules for public holidays ensures smooth transport, catering, and entertainment services, particularly for large-scale business conferences in the Middle East or high-end gala dinner planning.
- **Team-Building and Incentive Travel:** Leverage extended weekends for executive retreats or incentive travel UAE programs, blending business objectives with authentic cultural experiences.
