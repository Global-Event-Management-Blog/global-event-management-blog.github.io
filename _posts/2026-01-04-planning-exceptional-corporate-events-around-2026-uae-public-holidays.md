## Key 2026 UAE Public Holidays to Consider

While Islamic holidays follow the lunar calendar and may shift slightly, the below dates will shape group travel planning and event logistics for 2026:

- **New Year’s Day** (January 1): A global celebration, ideal for launching incentive travel UAE-wide or new corporate initiatives.
- **Eid Al Fitr** (Expected mid-March): Marking the end of Ramadan, this is a peak period for exclusive gatherings, team-building experiences, or private group events.
- **Arafat Day & Eid Al Adha** (Expected late May): The ‘Festival of Sacrifice’ offers a culturally rich window for immersive experiences and luxury hospitality.
- **Islamic New Year** (Expected late August): A contemplative holiday, suited for executive retreats or intimate networking sessions.
- **Prophet Muhammad’s Birthday** (Expected mid-September): Opportunities for thoughtful, respectful corporate events.
- **Commemoration Day & UAE National Day** (December 1-2): The pinnacle of national pride, ideal for high-end event production and unforgettable corporate galas.

*Note: Dates may vary; always confirm closer to the event.*
